Dimensionality

One neat thing about vector spaces is that some of them (if you’re lucky) have a notion of dimensionality. We say that a vector space is N-dimensional if there exists N “basis” vectors where any vector can be described as scaled sums of all of them, and that N is the lowest number of basis vectors needed.
